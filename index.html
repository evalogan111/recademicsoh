Ohio Boating License: A Complete Guide to Getting Certified

Ohio offers plenty of opportunities for boating on its scenic lakes and rivers, but before you hit the water, you may need an Ohio Boating License. This guide covers everything you need to know about the requirements, certification process, and important state boating laws.

Do You Need a Boating License in Ohio?

Ohio law requires certain individuals to complete a boater safety course and obtain a boater education card to operate a motorized vessel. According to the Ohio Department of Natural Resources (ODNR), if you were born on or after January 1, 1982, you must complete an approved boater education course to legally operate a boat with 10 horsepower or more.

Who Is Exempt?

You do not need an Ohio Boater Education Card if:

  • You were born before January 1, 1982.
  • You are operating a boat with less than 10 horsepower.
  • You are under direct supervision of someone who meets the boating education requirements.
  • You have successfully completed a proficiency exam in place of the boating course.

Even if you are exempt, taking a boating safety course is highly recommended to ensure a safe experience on the water.

Ohio Boating Laws and Safety Regulations

Age Requirements for Boating

  • Operators under 12 years old may not operate any personal watercraft (PWC) or vessel with more than 10 horsepower.
  • Individuals 12-15 years old may operate a motorized boat only if supervised by an adult (18+ years) who has met the boater education requirements.
  • Boaters 16 years and older can operate a vessel independently if they have completed a boater safety course.

Life Jacket Requirements

  • All boats must carry a U.S. Coast Guard-approved life jacket for every passenger.
  • Children under 10 years old must wear a life jacket while on a vessel under 18 feet.
  • Personal watercraft (jet ski) operators must wear a life jacket at all times.

Boating Under the Influence (BUI) Laws

  • Operating a boat under the influence of alcohol or drugs is illegal.
  • The legal blood alcohol concentration (BAC) limit is 0.08%, the same as for motor vehicles.
  • Penalties for Boating Under the Influence (BUI) can include fines, jail time, and loss of boating privileges.

Benefits of Getting an Ohio Boating License

  • Legal Compliance – Avoid fines and penalties by meeting state requirements.
  • Improved Safety – Learn how to handle emergencies and prevent accidents.
  • Lower Insurance Rates – Many insurance providers offer discounts for boaters with a safety certification.
  • Interstate Recognition – The Ohio Boater Education Card is accepted in many other states with mandatory boater education laws.
